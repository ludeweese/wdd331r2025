I wonder how they did that?

I came across three websites that made me think, "I wonder how they did that?" The first one I explored was the Apple website, where I was immediately drawn to the image slider on their homepage. It showcases multiple products and features by sliding images horizontally, creating an engaging visual experience. Inspired by this, I created a similar slider with the same effect.

Next, I want to highlight the video banner on the homepage of the BYU-Idaho website, which I visit daily. The video banner is beautifully integrated, and I especially enjoy how it adds dynamic motion to the page, creating a welcoming atmosphere for visitors.

Lastly, I checked out the Amazon Prime website and was impressed by their simple yet effective search bar. The interactive input bar stands out with a fun effect: when you click to type, the border around the bar smoothly changes to orange. This small detail adds a delightful, interactive touch to the user experience, and I loved how it added to the overall feel of the website.

Week 11

On the CSS Design Awards websiteI noticed a neat hover tooltip effect applied to the pictures of individuals. When you hover over the image of a person, a tooltip appears, revealing the person’s name and their role or brief description. For example, hovering over a person's image reveals details like "NEW SUSHISM is a media platform that redefines Japan's sushi culture, highlighting innovative styles." The tooltip smoothly fades in, providing an engaging and informative interaction. This effect helps convey more information without overwhelming the layout, making the site more interactive and dynamic.
